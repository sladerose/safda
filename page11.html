  • Industry administration: SAFDA operates within a regulated environment and as such, it is part of the South African Sugar Association (SASA), an administrative body that governs the South African sugar industry. SASA is established in terms of section 2 of the Sugar Act 9 of 1978. SASA is made up of sugarcane farmers, processors and refiners. SAFDA was formally recognised in with the amended Sugar Act Regulations that were published in October 2018. As a member of the industry we have to provide oversight and participate effectively in decision-making
  • Stakeholder relations and media: the function engages in strategic stakeholder engagement, ensuring that SAFDA is well represented within the industry, co-ordinating interactions with stakeholders at various levels including liaison with government ministries and parliament.
